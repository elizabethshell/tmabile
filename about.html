Biography & Contact


Tresha Mabile is a journalist and documentary filmmaker who has traveled to Iraq, Afghanistan and Pakistan to produce documentaries about America’s wars since 9/11.


Days after the fall of Baghdad in 2003, Tresha and her crew traveled throughout Iraq documenting as the country began to unravel for the Discovery/Times documentary After Saddam. She traveled back again in 2004 and 2005 to document the prosecutors building the legal case against Saddam Hussein for the Discovery/Times documentary The Case Against Saddam. In 2006, she produced a film for National Geographic, Saddam's Reign of Terror that documented how Saddam controlled and brutalized the population of his country.


In 2007 Tresha produced a film for CNN about the drug trade in Afghanistan, Narcostate. In 2009 Tresha traveled to the tribal regions of Pakistan and to Afghanistan for the National Geographic documentary Talibanistan. The film documented the return of the Taliban and U.S./NATO and Pakistani efforts to blunt their momentum.


In 2011 Tresha and her husband Peter Bergen traveled to Pakistan to produce The Last Days of Osama bin Laden, a documentary about the killing of bin Laden for National Geographic.


Tresha is also the producer of National Geographic’s Inside Hurricane Katrina, a two-hour investigation of the government’s botched response to the disaster.


In addition, she has produced more than a dozen other investigative and crime documentaries for A&E’s American Justice, Investigative Reports and Biography series. In 1999 she produced a documentary for the History Channel based on the New York Times bestseller Blind Man's Bluff, about submarine espionage during the Cold War.
